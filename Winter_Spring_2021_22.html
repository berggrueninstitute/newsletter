Peter Singer to Be Awarded 2021 Berggruen Prize at a Gala Ceremony in Los Angeles

Peter Singer was selected for his widely influential and intellectually rigorous work in rejuvenating utilitarianism as a part of academic philosophy and as a force for change in the world.

Winter-Spring 2021/2022

Renowned Thinker Antonio Damasio Named Chair of Berggruen Prize Jury

Taking over for Kwame Anthony Appiah as head of the Berggruen Prize Jury is Antonio Damasio, University Professor, David Dornsife Professor of Neuroscience, and Director of the Brain and Creativity Institute at the University of Southern California. The Jury also welcomes two new members: novelist Siri Hustvedt and political scientist Pratap Bhanu Mehta.

Berggruen Institute Announces Casa dei Tre Oci in Venice, Italy as Its European Center

Establishing a presence in the heart of Europe, the Institute will make Casa dei Tre Oci its center of European activity. In April, June, and December of 2022, Berggruen Arts & Culture will present a series of artist conversations in partnership with ArtReview at Casa dei Tre Oci.
 
 

Renovating Democracy Translated and Published in Spanish by NOLA Editors

Nicolas Berggruen and Nathan Gardels’ 2019 book is building a global readership with the new Spanish translation. Renovar La Democracia has received praise throughout Spain and Latin America with Mexico City as the next stop on the author's book tour.

China Center in 2021: Annual Report and Letter from Director Bing Song

The Berggruen China Center performs a unique role as a forum for dialogue across cultures and disciplines. Learn more about the China Center's achievements in 2021, including four books, new projects, and a cohort of six new Berggruen Fellows.
 

Youth Environment Service (YES) Campaign Expands Global Mission with Deliberative Assemblies and Opinion Survey

Deliberative assemblies throughout the UK, and a survey of seventeen democracies, revealed the global momentum behind the idea of investing in youth to fight climate change. These findings will fuel the YES campaign’s activities around the world in 2022, starting with deliberative assemblies in Athens and Venice.

Fast Company: How Cooperative Gig Economy Companies Managed to Flourish During the Pandemic

Berggruen Fellow Trebor Scholz discusses a new white paper published by the Future of Capitalism program. The paper gives local governments tools to challenge and reimagine digital capitalism around worker ownership.
 

Yael Eisenstat Discusses Facebook Whistleblower with Anderson Cooper

Berggruen Fellow and former Global Head of Elections Integrity Operations at Facebook Yael Eisenstat has been an authoritative voice on the impact of digital networks on democracy. Watch her on Anderson Cooper 360° discussing Frances Haugen’s bombshell testimony on Capitol Hill.

Transformations of the Human Graduates from Institute, Launches a School

With financial support from Reid Hoffman, the ToftH School welcomed its first cohort of students on December 1, 2021. In its independent, evolved form, ToftH will pioneer a new curriculum that brings philosophy, art, and technology into one practice.
 

Channeling New Vision: Fellows Study LA River as Model for Urban Futures
 

Staff member Christopher Eldred offers insights from a recent Berggruen Fellows journey on the LA river highlighting their tour from three perspectives: an augmented reality workshop with USC Professor Alex Robinson, Mia Lehrer’s studio, and a bike tour with local artist Steve Appleton.

Kim Stanley Robinson Talks Possible Worlds and “Optopia” on the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation (CBC)

The renowned science fiction author’s talk was the third installment of the Berggruen Institute’s “Possible Worlds” lecture series in partnership with UCLA. Listen to the talk on IDEAS, a CBC Radio documentary program.
 

Los Angeles Times Op-Ed: After 2021 Tumult, Here’s What It Will Take to Protect American Democracy

Nils Gilman, Vice President of Programs at the Berggruen Institute, speaks out about the rapidly unfolding threats to American democracy. Read his urgent guidance on what institutions and individuals must do to protect the integrity of our democratic process.

On the Loss of Dr. Paul Farmer, the 2020 Berggruen Prize Laureate
 

Berggruen Prize Jury and Institute leadership reflect on the passing of Dr. Farmer and how his work changed how the world thinks about public health, disease, and human rights.
