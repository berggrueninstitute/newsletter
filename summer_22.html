Berggruen Governance Index—A New Tool to Measure Governance

The 2022 Berggruen Governance Index is a major new analysis of 21st century global governance using data from a 20-year period to evaluate 134 national governments throughout the world. Hear Dawn Nakagawa explain the importance of the Index as an analytical tool.

Summer 2022

Nathan Gardels: Citizens’ Panels Show Path Forward for European Democracy

Berggruen Fellow Carsten Berg sees a bright future for deliberative democracy on the continent after observing randomly-selected citizens’ panels at the Conference on the Future of Europe. In Noema, our co-founder writes that these citizens’ panels could bolster the legitimacy of EU governance.

Yakov Feygin: Building a High-Performance Economy in a Time of COVID and Conflict

Our economy is built like a sports car, says Associate Director of the Future of Capitalism Yakov Feygin: capital-light and fun to zoom around in, so long as the roads are smooth. However, in a time of global turbulence we must rethink investment to focus on redundancy and resilience.

The Washington Post: The Troubled Paradox of US Democracy

Researchers Markus Lang and Edward Knudsen discuss the US–based Berggruen Governance Index findings with columnist Ishaan Tharoor. Steep declines in US quality of democracy and state capacity contrast sharply with rosy views of American governance uncovered by global opinion surveys.

Berggruen Institute Hosts East-West Dialogue at Casa dei Tre Oci in Venice, Italy

Confronting the deteriorating relationship between China and the West, the Berggruen Institute gathered global leaders, thinkers, and stakeholders for a conference in June at our center for European activities. Participants developed possible paths for dialogue on critica issues from the Ukraine to climate change.

ArtReview Series at Casa dei Tre Oci: Art in the Age of the Planetary

Read about ArtReview’s collaborative lecture series featuring conversations with artists Shubigi Rao and Wu Tsang; Los Angeles-based artist Sterling Ruby together with art historian Ester Coen and Berggruen Arts Artistic Director Mario Codognato. The series explores the intersections of philosophy, science, and cultures, and celebrates Tre Oci as a gathering place for artists, curators, and thinkers from across the world.
