Berggruen Institute Launches New Program Area: The Planetary

From climate change and biodiversity loss to tech risk and pandemic preparedness, many of today’s most urgent challenges exceed the boundaries of nations and the limits of human concerns. Going beyond the intellectual framework of the era of globalization, the Berggruen Institute’s program in the Planetary aims to develop and support new means and mechanisms to foster cooperation in an era of mutual interdependence not just with each other but with the biogeochemical processes of the Earth itself.

Noema Magazine Celebrates One Year Mark with New Issue: Planetary Realism

Summer 2021 marks one year since the launch of Noema Magazine, a crucial milestone for the new publication focused on exploring and amplifying transformative ideas. The second issue of Noema Magazine, Planetary Realism, shifts from the paradigms of globalization and geopolitics toward a concept of the “planetary” featuring content by Jerry Brown and Stewart Brand; Audrey Tang, Bruno Maçães, Suzanne Simard, Venkatesh Rao, and Mariana Mazzucato.

Living with Machines: Future Perspectives and Analysis
 

Hosted by the Berggruen Research Center at Peking University, Living with Machines: Future Perspectives and Analysis featured discussions with Jianqiao Ge, Qiaoying Lu, Yi Zeng, Weiwen Duan, and Sebastian Sunday Greve focusing on both the theoretical and practical aspects of humans living with machines. Key questions explored the assessment of cognitive and affective capacities in humans and non-humans, and their theoretical models, as well as the relevant practical strategies both in the lab and in everyday life. University of California (UC) Press and Berggruen Institute Great Transformation Series Publishes The Human Scaffold

Berggruen Fellow Josh Berson’s new book The Human Scaffold: How Not to Design Your Way Out of a Climate Crisis examines how a reframing of scholarship, policy, and culture away from material technology is a prerequisite for solving climate change and other challenges. A new summer event series explores these themes beginning July 28, 2021.
