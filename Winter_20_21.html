Dr. Farmer Awarded 2020 Berggruen Prize for
Philosophy & Culture

The Berggruen Prize Jury selects Dr. Paul Farmer, medical anthropologist and co-founder of Partners In Health, for his work in connecting the philosophical articulation of human rights with the practical pursuit of health. The work and ideas of Dr. Paul Farmer will be celebrated in a virtual talk moderated by our event partner BBC News World Service in late spring 2021.

Nicolas Berggruen: Reflections on a
Turbulent Year

The Institute was founded to study the great transformations affecting our world and this year has accelerated our work on all fronts. While we work to adapt and respond, one thing remains clear: governance matters at every level and it continues to guide both our research and activities to develop new ideas for renewing democracy, rethinking capitalism, and creating tools for planetary dialogue.

Berggruen Institute Proposes the Creation of Youth Environment Service (YES)

YES is a campaign to build awareness and support around youth-led programs taking action to mitigate the impact of global climate change, and create jobs and service opportunities for young people in the midst of the economic downturn created by the COVID-19 pandemic. The program aims to cultivate a new generation of diverse, purpose-driven leadership, and civic-minded participation.

Renowned Landscape Architect Mia Lehrer Joins the Design and Environmental Team for Berggruen Institute Scholars Campus

Known for her interdisciplinary nature projects, Studio-MLA founder Mia Lehrer will work to preserve and restore the 450-acre hilltop site; expand and improve public trails; and develop access to natural areas through climate-resilient design.

Berggruen Institute Commits $3 Million to Support UCLA Research Partnership

The UCLA Luskin School of Public Affairs will produce and disseminate the Berggruen Governance Index which evaluates countries based on their quality of political and administrative governance. For policymakers and policy analysts, the Index will serve as a much‐needed tool for grasping how governance relates to social and economic progress in various political contexts.
