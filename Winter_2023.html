Kojin Karatani Awarded 2022 Berggruen Prize for Philosophy & Culture

The Berggruen Prize Jury selects preeminent philosopher and literary critic Kojin Karatani for his radically original contributions to modern philosophy, the history of philosophy, and political thinking in a time of global crisis. The first Asian laureate of the Prize, Karatani’s ideas will be celebrated in late April in Tokyo, Japan together with Asahi Shimbun and BBC.

Future Humans Theme Explores the Question: “What Will Life Become?”

Claire Isabel Webb is leading The Future Humans Theme at the Institute. Webb will explore how the concept of “life” is undergoing meteoric change from the microbial to the planetary. She is kicking off a two-year collaboration with the Berggruen Institute’s Jonathan Blake on the Multispecies Constitution Project; organizing a seminar-style writing retreat led by renowned science fiction author Ken Liu and Stanley Chen for a project “Vaster” than Empires”; and radically rethinking concepts of consciousness, intelligence, agency, and liveliness itself through the Embodied Machines project. Central to all of this work is the question: What Will Life Become? Read the first of Webb‘s essays that respond to this profound inquiry for the Future Humans Theme through its tentacles of exploration.
